   Introduction: Be Able to Understand the Structure and Function of the Circulatory System
   Understanding the structure and function of the circulatory system is essential for recognising how
   blood, oxygen, and nutrients are transported throughout the body. The circulatory system consists
   mainly of the heart, blood vessels, and blood, working together to support vital bodily functions. It
   delivers oxygen to the muscles during exercise, removes waste products like carbon dioxide, and helps
   regulate body temperature. Knowledge of this system is crucial in health and fitness, as it helps explain
   how the body responds to physical activity and why cardiovascular health is so important.
   The student should begin by stating that the heart is a vital organ in the circulatory system, responsible
   for pumping blood throughout the body to deliver oxygen and nutrients and remove waste.
